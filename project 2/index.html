A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missil Man of India

The Missile Man of India is a popular nickname for Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, who was a renowned Indian scientist and politician. He played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs, and also served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was widely respected and admired for his vision, leadership, and humility

Biographies

  • He was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a poor Muslim family.
  • He graduated in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and then studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • He joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958 and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969.
  • He also supervised the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear-weapon state.
  • He was also a prolific writer and a motivational speaker. He authored several books, such as Wings of Fire, India 2020, and Ignited Minds.
